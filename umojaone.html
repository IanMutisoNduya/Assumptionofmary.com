ASSUMPTION OF MARY HISTORY.

The history of this church dates back to 1976 when Umoja estate was still under development, the Christians used to attend Sunday Mass in either Jericho or BuruBuru under the Maryknoll Fathers and Kariobangi under the Verona Fathers. Then in 1977, Fr Romeo and a few Christians started a two-week outreach program within the estate, this sensitization resulted in mass being celebrated within Umoja one Primary School, other denominations also began using the school resulting to the cancellation of the agreement. We were later allowed to use the car shed of the estate developers, during this period Fr Romeo had submitted an application form to be allocated a plot for the Catholic Church Community Centre, There was a lot of delay in the allocation.

One Sunday after Mass Fr Romeo requested the Christians to pray for the intervention of the Blessed Virgin Mary and tasked one of the Altar boys during the time Mr Gabriel Mbugua, to bury a miraculous medal in the ground he stated “ let us see whether Jesus Christ Son of Mary will let rain fall on his mother's image” , surprisingly the plot where the medal was buried was allocated to the church, it is until today we gather for Mass. Indeed, our God is a God of miracles.

The first Sub parish council was formed in 1978 led by Mr Lazarus Maigua, and his team, Michael Munyi, Mrs Maina and Mr Kamanda under the supervision of Fr Romeo, Then the building of our first Church begun, they dedicated themselves in ensuring the Church ground breaking commenced. We have experienced tremendous growth under different leadership and guidance from Maryknoll missionaries the diocesan Parish Priests and assistant priests and different Parish councils. The evidence is as you can see today.

We became a parish in 1983 under the Maryknoll Fathers until 1997 when the parish was placed under the archdiocese of Nairobi with Fr Michael Komu as the parish Priest assisted by Fr Anselim Kamuyu and Fr Charles Kanai we had outstations of Embakasi, and Donholm who are now individual parishes. We have grown and we have two more outstations St. Veronica in Umoja Two and St. Teresa of Calcutta Tena.

We pioneered CMA in Nairobi that was then called CMM (catholic Men Movement) an idea borrowed from the Diocese of Nakuru. We have currently 87 Jumuiya's, PMC, Liturgy ministry, Lectors, needy, family life, Health, MYM, YCA, YSC, Youth, CWA, CJPD and Choirs. we have two outstations St. Veronica Umoja 2 and St. Teresa of Calcutta Tena Estate. We have two Schools, we have three religious communities, the Medical Missionary Sisters, little daughters of St. Paul, the CMM Brothers community and the CDP collage, we have 3 masses in the parish. Three masses at St. Veronica and Two Masses in St. Teresa of Calcutta. We have five catechists that take care of our catechesis, we have eight staff who take care of our Church grounds.

We now have a new Church the ground breaking took place in December of 2018. It has taken us 5yrs and 172 million for us to reach here we can't take it for granted. we thank God for this far he has brought us. We have on average a population of 5000 Christians hence the need for a new Church. The construction, consecration and dedication of this New Church to the Assumption Of Mary is a milestone for our history and may many people find Christ though Mother Mary when they come to worship and pray in this Church.

GOD BLESS US ALL.
